# The Review of Korean Studies (RKS)

Peer-reviewed journal of Korean studies published by The Academy of Korean Studies (한국학중앙연구원), covering Korean history, culture, religion, and society across the humanities and social sciences.

---

## 2002

- [Walraven (2002) *RKS*](../references/Walraven_2002_RKS.md) — "Weavers of Ritual: How Shamans Achieve Their Aims" — applies the rational actor axiom (Stark & Finke) and a focus on ritual *practice* (vs. reified "tradition") to Korean shamanic *kut* in the Hwanghae and Seoul styles; ethnographic fieldwork (Seoul/Inch'ŏn, mostly 1999–2000); argues the effective *kut* is a psychological transaction organized around relief (*shiwon-hada*), individualized through food/cloth/money, divinatory feats, and *kongsu* oracles; the shaman as "equilibrist" balancing fixed structure and personalization via the *putt'a*/*p'ulda* metaphors
