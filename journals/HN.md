# Human Nature (HN)

Interdisciplinary journal publishing research on the biological, evolutionary, and anthropological foundations of human behavior. Published by Springer.

---

## 2021

- [Boyer (2021) *HN*](../references/Boyer_2021_HN.md) — "Deriving Features of Religions in the Wild" — Good Source model: epistemic vigilance + threat-detection psychology → communication dynamics predicting five features of wild (pre-doctrinal) religious traditions; explains production (motivation to propose threat-related explanations) not just consumption; five predictions on misfortune focus, inscrutability, specificity, specialization, and essentialism of specialists; purely theoretical
- [Hong & Henrich (2021) *HN*](../references/Hong_Henrich_2021_HN.md) — "The Cultural Evolution of Epistemic Practices: The Case of Divination" — divination as epistemic technology; Bayesian agent-based simulation of belief formation; three overestimation mechanisms (strong priors, underreporting of negative evidence, misinferring belief from observed behavior); cultural group selection account for spread of scientific epistemology; ethnographic fieldwork among Yi and Wa in SW China (N=76 interviews; N=47 perceived-accuracy survey)

## 2003

- [Sosis (2003) *HN*](../references/Sosis_2003_HN.md) — "Why Aren't We All Hutterites? Costly Signaling Theory and Religious Behavior" — graphical model formalizing Irons's costly signaling theory of ritual; proximate mechanism (ritual → belief via cognitive dissonance/self-perception) explains differential opportunity costs between believers and skeptics; five testable predictions derived
