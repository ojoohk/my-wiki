# Concept / Theory / Method Index

Alphabetical. Each entry links to the reference(s) that introduce it.

---

## C

- **coalitional signals** — ritualized gestures or markings indicating membership in a specific cooperative ensemble or joint action; require shared intentionality and recursion → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)
- **commitment signals** — costly self-inflicted behaviors signaling graded obligation to a risky joint action; honest via costly signaling mechanism → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)
- **complex signaling systems** (ritual as) — ritual signals form a functional unit that is redundant, degenerate, and pluripotent (Barker et al., 2019; Hebets et al., 2016) → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)
- **cooperative signaling platform** — the hypothesis that collective ritual's primary function is communicating cooperative intentions via multiple signal types → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)
- **costly signaling theory** (in ritual/religion) — Grafen (1990) / Zahavi & Zahavi (1999) framework; applied to commitment signals in ritual to explain honest signaling of cooperative intent → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)

## M

- **mutualistic cooperation** — cooperative arrangement where both parties benefit from the joint action; primary type facilitated by ritual signaling → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)

## O

- **ontogenetic ritualization** — process by which dyads shorten idiosyncratic gesture sequences into simple placeholders through repeated interaction (Tomasello et al., 1997) → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)

## R

- **ritual (Rappaport definition)** — "the performance of more or less invariant sequences of formal acts and utterances not entirely encoded by the performers" (Rappaport, 1999, p. 24) → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)

## S

- **similarity signals** — ritualized mimicry or synchronous movement indicating shared interests / mutual relatedness; earliest of three ritual signal types (~1.76 Ma H. ergaster) → [Lang & Kundt (2023) *RBB*](references/Lang_Kundt_2023_RBB.md)
